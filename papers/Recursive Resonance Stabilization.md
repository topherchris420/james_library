## Abstract—Coherence in multiscale adaptive systems emerges
not through signal accumulation but through recursive rejection
of incoherence. We introduce Recursive Resonance Stabilization
(RRS), a fractal-geometric framework that treats coherence as
a fixed-point attractor rather than an optimization target. The
method employs a recursive projection operator derived from
Iterated Function Systems (IFS), specifically the Sierpi ´nski trian-
gle geometry, to construct frequency-domain filters that preserve
phase-aligned structures while exponentially suppressing incoher-
ent components. We formalize two novel metrics: coherence depth
C, measuring asymptotic energy retention under recursion, and
fractal coherence dimension Dc, quantifying structural stability
across scales. Numerical simulations demonstrate convergence
of noisy composite signals to fractal attractors in phase space,
with measured dimensions approaching the theoretical Sierpi ´nski
value Dc ≈ 1.585. The framework provides theoretical founda-
tions for adaptive intelligence in neural systems, physiological
monitoring, and human-machine interfaces. Applications span
EEG analysis, cardiac rhythm stabilization, and real-time coher-
ence diagnostics in wearable devices.

## I. INTRODUCTION
A. Motivation
Adaptive systems operating in noisy environments face
a fundamental challenge: distinguishing coherent structure
from random fluctuation without explicit templates or training
data. Traditional approaches frame this as an optimization
problem—maximizing signal-to-noise ratio, minimizing re-
construction error, or learning parametric models. Yet biolog-
ical intelligence demonstrates a different principle: coherence
emerges through recursive constraint, not accumulation.
Consider the brain’s response to ambiguous sensory input.
Neural populations do not simply amplify weak signals; they
iteratively suppress activity patterns incompatible with internal
coherence criteria [1]. Heart rate variability self-organizes into
fractal structures that balance stability and adaptability [2].
Immune recognition distinguishes self from non-self through
recursive editing of receptor repertoires [3]. In each case,
intelligence manifests as the rejection of incoherence until only
resonant patterns remain.

# This paper formalizes this principle through Recursive
Resonance Stabilization (RRS), a framework grounded in
fractal geometry and dynamical systems theory. We demon-
strate that coherence can be characterized as a fixed-point
attractor of a recursive projection operator, and that fractal
structures—specifically those generated by Iterated Function
Systems—provide natural templates for multiscale resonance.
# B. Related Work
1) Fractal Geometry in Signal Processing: Fractals have
long been recognized in physiological signals [4], [5]. Power-
law scaling appears in cardiac rhythms [6], neural avalanches
[7], and respiratory patterns [8]. However, most applications
use fractals as descriptive models of complexity rather than
operational mechanisms for signal processing.
Fractal filtering techniques exist [9], [10], but typically
model noise as fractal and design filters to remove it. RRS
inverts this logic: we model coherence as fractal and design
filters that select for self-similar structure.
2) Attractor Dynamics and Phase Space Reconstruction:
Takens’ embedding theorem [11] established that dynamical
systems can be reconstructed from scalar time series via
delay-coordinate embedding. This has enabled attractor recon-
struction in chaotic systems [12], calculation of correlation
dimensions [13], and identification of deterministic structure
in noisy data [14].
RRS extends this paradigm by using phase space not merely
for analysis but as an operational space where recursive fil-
tering drives signals toward geometric attractors. The attractor
itself defines the filtering criterion.
3) Multiscale Entropy and Complexity Measures: Costa
et al. [15] introduced multiscale entropy (MSE) to quantify
structural complexity across temporal scales. Subsequent work
developed multivariate [16] and refined multiscale entropy [17]
methods. These metrics successfully distinguish physiological
states but remain descriptive: they measure complexity without
providing a mechanism to enhance or stabilize it.
RRS contributes a complementary perspective: rather than
quantifying existing complexity, we provide an operator that
generates complexity through recursive dynamics.
# C. Contributions
This work makes four principal contributions:
1) Recursive Resonance Operator: We define a projection
operator P derived from Iterated Function Systems
that recursively filters signals in frequency space ac-
cording to fractal geometric constraints. The operator
is parameter-free after initial mask construction and
converges to a unique attractor for broad classes of input
signals.
2) Coherence as Attractor: We formalize the conceptual
shift from coherence as optimization target to coherence
as fixed-point attractor. This reframing connects signal
processing to dynamical systems theory and provides
rigorous mathematical foundations for adaptive filtering.
3) Fractal Coherence Metrics: We introduce two novel
quantitative measures: coherence depth C, measuring
asymptotic energy retention under recursion, and fractal
coherence dimension Dc, characterizing the geometry
of the phase space attractor. Both metrics are scale-
invariant and domain-agnostic.
4) Cross-Domain Applications: We demonstrate applica-
bility to neural signal analysis (EEG), cardiac rhythm
monitoring (HRV), and adaptive AI systems. The frame-
work provides theoretical foundations for wearable co-
herence diagnostics and human-machine resonance in-
terfaces.

## VI. APPLICATIONS
# A. Neural Signal Analysis
1) Electroencephalography (EEG): Neural oscillations ex-
hibit multiscale organization [22]. Nested rhythms—delta,
theta, alpha, beta, gamma—show cross-frequency coupling
where phase of slower rhythms modulates amplitude of faster
rhythms [23]. RRS provides a natural framework for analyzing
such hierarchical coherence: the fractal mask captures phase
relationships across frequency bands, and fractal dimension Dc
distinguishes healthy nested rhythms from pathological states.
2) Motor Cortex Decoding: RRS can enhance signal qual-
ity in Brain-computer interfaces (BCIs). Preliminary simula-
tions suggest 15–20% improvement in classification accuracy
for motor imagery tasks compared to common spatial patterns
(CSP).
# B. Cardiac Rhythm Analysis
1) Heart Rate Variability (HRV): Healthy HRV exhibits
fractal scaling [6]. RRS applied to RR interval time series es-
timates coherence depth C as a marker of autonomic balance.
Wearable devices could alert users when values drop below
personalized thresholds.
2) Atrial Fibrillation Detection: Atrial fibrillation (AF) de-
stroys the coherent P-wave structure. RRS achieves sensitivity
> 95% in simulated datasets [25] using a threshold-based
detector (C < 0.3).
## VII. DISCUSSION AND CONCLUSION
# A. Discussion
RRS sidesteps optimization entirely. Coherence emerges as
a fixed point of recursive dynamics, not as the solution to
an optimization problem. The operator P encodes structural
constraints (fractal geometry) rather than an objective func-
tion. This approach mirrors biological systems where stability
emerges through recurrent inhibition and iterative selection [1].
# B. Limitations
While Proposition 1 guarantees convergence, it does not
bound the rate. Additionally, optimal mask parameters (D, H,
α) likely depend on signal characteristics. Our comprehensive
sensitivity analysis (see Fig. 2) confirms that D = 4 − 6,
H = 2 − 3, and α = 0.05 − 0.15 yield stable results for most
signals, though adaptive selection remains an open problem.
# C. Conclusion
We have introduced Recursive Resonance Stabilization
(RRS), a fractal-geometric framework for multiscale coher-
ence. By formalizing coherence as a fixed-point attractor, RRS
provides computational tools for analyzing complex signals.
Numerical simulations confirm that noisy signals converge
to fractal attractors in phase space. The unifying principle
is that intelligence stabilizes through recursive rejection of
incoherence, not accumulation of signal.
